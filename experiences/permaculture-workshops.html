Permaculture Workshops: Learn Regenerative Farming in Bali and Mexico

Get your hands in the soil and learn the farming practices that heal the Earth. From composting and food forest design in Bali's rice terraces to ancient milpa polyculture in Oaxaca, these workshops transform how you think about food, land, and your relationship to both.

The Practice

Permaculture is not a gardening technique. It is a way of seeing the world.

"When you learn to observe how a forest grows itself, with no irrigation, no fertilizer, no weeding, you start to understand that nature already has the answers. Permaculture is simply learning to ask the right questions and then getting out of nature's way."

— Wayan Suartika, Permaculture Teacher, Ubud

Permaculture, a design system that mimics natural ecosystems to create abundant, self-sustaining food systems, has grown from a fringe idea into one of the most practical responses to industrial agriculture's environmental devastation. In our workshops, you do not just learn theory. You build compost systems, plant food forests, design water harvesting landscapes, and eat the food grown by previous workshop participants. The learning is circular, just like the systems you are studying.

Bali, Indonesia

Tropical Permaculture in Ubud's Rice Terraces

The Setting

Nestled among Ubud's famous rice terraces, the Bali workshop campus is itself a living permaculture demonstration site. Over 200 edible species grow in layered food forests. Bamboo structures house classrooms, composting stations, and seed banks. The subak water temple irrigation system, a UNESCO-recognized traditional practice, feeds the surrounding rice paddies, demonstrating how indigenous water management predates modern permaculture by centuries.

Curriculum Highlights

Tropical food forest design with seven-layer planting systems. Hot composting, vermicomposting, and bokashi fermentation. Bamboo construction and natural building. Rice terrace ecology and the subak irrigation system. Tropical seed saving and heritage variety preservation. Aquaponics and integrated fish-plant systems. Natural pest management using companion planting and beneficial insects.

Instructors

Balinese permaculture teachers who combine traditional Tri Hita Karana philosophy (harmony with God, community, and nature) with modern permaculture design principles. Guest lectures from visiting international permaculture designers. All instruction is available in English and Indonesian.

Daily Rhythm

Mornings begin with hands-on garden work: planting, composting, harvesting. After a communal organic breakfast prepared from the garden, classroom sessions cover design theory. Afternoons return to practical application: building compost systems, designing water features, or grafting fruit trees. Evenings feature community meals and discussion circles under bamboo pavilions.

Mexico

Ancient Wisdom Meets Regenerative Design in Oaxaca

Milpa Polyculture

Learn the milpa system, the 7,000-year-old Mesoamerican polyculture of corn, beans, and squash (the Three Sisters) that remains one of the most productive and sustainable farming systems ever developed. Understand how nitrogen-fixing beans feed the corn, how squash leaves shade the soil, and how this system produces complete nutrition without synthetic inputs.

Dryland Permaculture

Oaxaca's semi-arid climate demands water-wise design. Learn to build swales, berms, and rain gardens that capture and infiltrate every drop of rainfall. Study ancient Zapotec terracing techniques that prevent erosion on steep hillsides. Practice greywater recycling systems that turn kitchen water into garden irrigation.

Seed Sovereignty

Mexico is the origin center of corn, with over 59 native varieties still cultivated by indigenous farmers. Workshop sessions on seed saving cover selection, drying, storage, and sharing. You learn why seed sovereignty is not just an agricultural issue but a cultural and political act of resistance against corporate seed monopolies.

Mezcal Agroforestry

Explore how sustainable mezcal production integrates with permaculture principles. Visit traditional palenques where agave is grown in polyculture with native trees, creating agroforestry systems that produce spirits, food, and timber while building soil and sequestering carbon.

Programs

Workshop Formats

Choose the depth that fits your schedule and learning goals.

Weekend Intensive (2-3 Days)

Perfect for travelers with limited time. Covers permaculture fundamentals: observation, soil health, composting basics, and kitchen garden design. You leave with enough knowledge to start a productive garden at home. Includes accommodation and organic meals. Available in both Bali and Mexico.

One-Week Immersion (7 Days)

A deeper dive into hands-on permaculture. Build a complete compost system, design a small food forest, learn water harvesting, and practice seed saving. Extended time allows for observation of systems over multiple days. Community meals and evening discussions deepen the learning. Available in both locations.

Permaculture Design Certificate (14 Days)

The internationally recognized 72-hour PDC covers the full permaculture design methodology. Climate analysis, landscape reading, zone and sector planning, earthworks, water systems, food forests, animal integration, community design, and a final design project. Graduate with the knowledge to design sustainable systems for any climate. Available seasonally.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

No prior experience is required. Our permaculture workshops are designed for complete beginners through intermediate practitioners. The hands-on curriculum starts with fundamental principles of observation, soil health, and water management before progressing to design systems and implementation. Experienced gardeners and farmers will find advanced modules on food forest layering, mycelium networks, and tropical polyculture systems.

Workshop durations vary by program. Weekend intensives run 2-3 days and cover permaculture fundamentals. One-week immersions provide deeper hands-on experience with composting, garden design, and water systems. The full Permaculture Design Certificate (PDC) course runs 72 hours over two weeks and covers the complete permaculture design methodology. All programs include accommodation in eco-lodges and organic meals.

The Bali workshops focus on tropical permaculture systems including rice terrace management, bamboo construction, tropical food forests, and the integration of Balinese water temple irrigation traditions. The Mexico workshops emphasize milpa polyculture, dryland water harvesting, native seed preservation, and indigenous Mesoamerican farming knowledge. Both programs teach universal permaculture principles applied to their specific bioregion.

You will learn practical skills applicable anywhere: composting methods (hot compost, vermicompost, bokashi), water harvesting and greywater recycling, soil building through mulching and cover cropping, food forest design principles, companion planting, seed saving techniques, and natural pest management. You will also learn observation and design methodology that helps you apply permaculture thinking to any landscape or climate.

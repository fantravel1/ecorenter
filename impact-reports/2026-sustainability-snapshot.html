Carbon Footprint Analysis

The network-wide average carbon footprint decreased to 12.4 kg CO2e per guest-night, an 8.1% improvement from 13.5 kg CO2e in 2025. This compares favorably to the global hotel industry average of 25-35 kg CO2e per guest-night.

Performance distribution:

  • Top quartile (37 properties): Average 5.8 kg CO2e per guest-night
  • Second quartile: Average 9.7 kg CO2e per guest-night
  • Third quartile: Average 14.3 kg CO2e per guest-night
  • Bottom quartile: Average 19.2 kg CO2e per guest-night

The primary drivers of carbon reduction were increased solar panel installations (27 properties added capacity in 2025), improved building insulation (particularly in temperate-climate properties), and transition from diesel generators to battery storage systems (14 properties).

Carbon offset partnerships: 89 verified properties participate in Gold Standard or Verra VCS offset programs, collectively offsetting 4,200 tonnes CO2e annually. However, we emphasize that offsetting is complementary to, not a substitute for, direct emission reductions.

Water Conservation Progress

Average water consumption decreased to 287 liters per guest-night, a 12% improvement from 326 liters in 2025. The global hotel industry average remains 500-800 liters per guest-night.

Key achievements:

  • 42 properties now operate rainwater harvesting systems, collecting an estimated 18 million liters annually
  • 67 properties recycle graywater for irrigation, reducing freshwater demand by an average of 30%
  • All 147 properties have installed low-flow fixtures in guest rooms
  • Linen reuse programs are operational at 134 properties

Honest assessment: 12 properties in arid regions still exceed 400 liters per guest-night, primarily due to swimming pool evaporation and irrigation of non-native landscaping. These properties have committed to conversion to native drought-resistant landscaping by 2027.

Waste Management

The network average waste diversion rate reached 78%, up from 73% in 2025. Twenty-three properties now achieve over 90% diversion from landfill.

Single-use plastic elimination is complete at 89 of 147 properties (61%). The remaining 58 properties have active elimination plans targeting completion by end of 2026.

Renewable Energy Adoption

Renewable energy now accounts for 64% of total energy consumption across the network, up from 52% in 2025. This includes 41% generated on-site and 23% purchased through renewable agreements or clean grid electricity.

Thirty-one properties now operate at 100% renewable energy. Twenty-seven properties added solar capacity during 2025, with a combined installation of 2.4 MW of new solar photovoltaic systems. Four properties installed micro-hydro systems, and two implemented geothermal heating.

Community Economic Impact

Verified properties collectively directed $4.2 million to community development in 2025, a 22% increase from $3.4 million in 2024.

  • Education funding: $1.8 million supporting 47 schools and educational programs
  • Healthcare support: $1.1 million funding 12 community health clinics
  • Infrastructure: $0.8 million for roads, water systems, and community facilities
  • Cultural preservation: $0.5 million supporting 23 cultural programs

The average local employment ratio across the network is 84%, with 52% of management positions held by local community members. The management representation gap remains an area for improvement.

Where We Need to Improve

Transparency means reporting shortcomings alongside successes:

  • Scope 3 reporting: Only 23% of properties currently track full value chain emissions. We are developing standardized tools to support broader Scope 3 reporting by 2027.
  • Food waste: Organic waste remains the single largest waste category. Pre-consumer food waste averages 0.3 kg per guest-night, which can be reduced through better demand forecasting and menu optimization.
  • Local management representation: While 84% of all staff are local, only 52% of management is local. Properties are implementing management training and succession planning programs.
  • Guest travel emissions: The largest carbon impact for most guests is the flight to the destination, which is outside property control. We are partnering with offset programs and promoting slow travel alternatives.

Our Commitment to Transparency

The sustainable tourism industry has a credibility problem. Too many organizations publish glossy sustainability reports filled with aspirational language and cherry-picked success stories. Real impact reporting requires something harder: publishing the numbers, including the uncomfortable ones.

EcoRenter impact reports present verified data from our network of eco-resorts and sustainable properties. We report what is working, what is not, and where the industry needs to improve. Every metric is sourced, every methodology is disclosed, and every limitation is acknowledged.

These reports serve two purposes: they help travelers understand the real impact of their booking choices, and they hold the industry accountable to measurable standards rather than marketing claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Data-driven publications measuring the real-world effects of eco-tourism across our verified properties. They cover sustainability metrics, community economic impact, and biodiversity conservation with published, verifiable data.

Data is collected from verified properties through standardized reporting, third-party certification audits, on-site verification, satellite imagery, community surveys, and ecological surveys. All data undergoes validation before inclusion.

Transparency is the foundation of trust. The antidote to greenwashing is published, verifiable data. Impact reports hold EcoRenter and verified properties accountable and demonstrate to travelers that their choices have real positive effects.

The annual Sustainability Snapshot is published in January. Community Impact and Biodiversity reports are updated biannually. Property-level metric dashboards update quarterly. Historical data is preserved for trend analysis.

Properties must first complete EcoRenter verification, requiring auditable sustainability data, recognized certifications, and demonstrated community and environmental commitment. Verified properties are automatically included in aggregate reporting.

Data draws from independently audited certification results and is cross-referenced with public sources. Methodology limitations and data gaps are disclosed transparently. Our goal is honest reporting, not marketing material.

Our Selection Criteria

Not every beautiful place deserves the "eco-destination" label.

The travel industry loves calling any destination with a palm tree "eco-friendly." We take a different approach. Every destination on EcoRenter passes a four-pillar evaluation: verifiable environmental policy at the national level, a critical mass of independently verified eco-resorts, active conservation programs with measurable outcomes, and infrastructure that supports sustainable long-stay travel. A destination that scores well on scenery but poorly on policy never makes the cut.

Our Phase 1 countries represent the world's most concentrated pockets of genuine sustainability practice. They span four continents, multiple climate zones, and diverse cultural approaches to environmental stewardship. From Costa Rica's pioneering payment-for-ecosystem-services model to Kenya's community conservancy system, each destination offers unique lessons in how tourism and conservation can reinforce each other.

Transparency First

How We Evaluate Destinations

Every destination and every resort within it is evaluated through our evidence-based system. Here is how it works at the destination level.

National Policy Assessment

We evaluate each country's environmental legislation, protected area percentages, renewable energy adoption, and enforcement track record. Strong policy does not guarantee good practice, but it creates the framework for it.

Eco-Resort Density

A destination must have at least 200 verified eco-resorts to be listed. High density means more traveler choice and competitive pressure for genuine sustainability, not just marketing claims.

Conservation Outcomes

We track measurable conservation results: species recovery data, deforestation rates, coral reef health, water quality trends. Destinations must show net positive environmental trajectories, not just aspirational targets.

Community Benefit

Tourism should enrich local communities, not extract from them. We assess local employment rates, fair wage standards, indigenous rights protections, and community ownership models in each destination.

Nomad Infrastructure

Internet reliability, co-working availability, visa accessibility, healthcare quality, and cost-of-living data for travelers planning extended sustainable stays of one month or longer.

Data Transparency

Destinations with open environmental data, published sustainability reports, and accessible monitoring systems score higher. Transparency is the foundation of trust.

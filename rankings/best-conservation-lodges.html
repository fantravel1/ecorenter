Conservation Ranking

Best Conservation Lodges Worldwide

The top 15 lodges where your stay directly funds wildlife conservation. From anti-poaching patrols on African savannahs to marine research stations on coral reefs, these lodges prove that tourism can be conservation's most powerful funding mechanism.

How We Evaluate Conservation Lodges

Each lodge is assessed on: percentage of revenue directly allocated to conservation, documented conservation outcomes (species recovery, habitat restoration, anti-poaching effectiveness), scientific research contributions, community conservation programs, operational sustainability, and transparency of impact reporting. Only lodges with verified, measurable conservation impact are included.

1. Ol Pejeta Conservancy Lodges, Kenya

Focus: Rhino, chimpanzee, and large mammal conservation

Ol Pejeta is home to the last two northern white rhinos on Earth and the largest black rhino sanctuary in East Africa. Every guest night directly funds 24/7 armed ranger patrols, a world-class veterinary facility, and the Sweetwaters Chimpanzee Sanctuary. The conservancy protects 360 km2 of critical habitat and has achieved near-zero poaching through technology-assisted security. Community programs employ 1,200+ local people and support surrounding schools and healthcare.

Conservation Impact
98

2. Lapa Rios Lodge, Costa Rica

Focus: Tropical rainforest and scarlet macaw conservation

Lapa Rios protects a 1,000-acre private rainforest reserve on the Osa Peninsula, one of the most biodiverse places on Earth. The lodge operates a wildlife monitoring program that has documented the recovery of scarlet macaw populations, spider monkey populations, and numerous frog species. 100% of lodge profits fund conservation and community education programs including the local school's environmental curriculum.

Conservation Impact
96

3. Segera Retreat, Kenya

Focus: Wildlife corridor and community conservation

Segera manages 50,000 acres in the Laikipia Plateau, a critical wildlife corridor connecting Mount Kenya to the northern rangelands. The retreat funds anti-poaching operations, wildlife research, and the Zeitz Foundation's conservation programs. Black rhino, elephant, and African wild dog populations have recovered significantly since conservation management began. The arts program generates income for local communities through cultural preservation.

Conservation Impact
95

4. Inkaterra Reserva Amazonica, Peru

Focus: Amazonian biodiversity research and habitat protection

Inkaterra operates one of the most productive field research stations in the Amazon, having contributed to the discovery of dozens of new species. The lodge's canopy walkway provides researchers and guests access to the forest crown. Conservation programs include spectacled bear monitoring, giant river otter habitat protection, and a native tree reforestation program that has planted over 500,000 trees. Scientific publications from the lodge's research contribute to global understanding of Amazonian ecology.

Conservation Impact
94

5. Singita Lodges, Tanzania/South Africa/Rwanda

Focus: Large-scale habitat restoration and community partnership

Singita's conservation model spans over 1 million acres across three countries. In the Grumeti Reserve (Tanzania), the company funds anti-poaching for 350,000 acres and has achieved dramatic wildlife recovery. In Kruger (South Africa), the Lebombo concession demonstrates luxury conservation at scale. Revenue from high-end tourism directly funds the Singita Lowveld Trust and community development programs that create economic alternatives to poaching.

Conservation Impact
93

6. Chumbe Island Coral Park, Zanzibar

Focus: Coral reef marine conservation

Chumbe Island operates the first marine protected area in Tanzania, protecting a pristine coral reef ecosystem with over 200 species of hard coral and 400+ fish species. The eco-lodge runs entirely on solar power and rainwater harvesting. Conservation rangers patrol the marine sanctuary daily, and the education program has trained thousands of local students in marine ecology. Scientific monitoring has documented coral recovery in areas previously damaged by destructive fishing.

7. Mashpi Lodge, Ecuador

Focus: Cloud forest conservation and species discovery

Mashpi Lodge protects 2,700 acres of Choco cloud forest in northwest Ecuador, a region recognized as one of the world's biodiversity hotspots. The lodge's Life Center conducts ongoing research into orchids, hummingbirds, amphibians, and insects. New species continue to be discovered on the reserve. A butterfly farm breeds and releases endangered species, and community forestry programs incentivize habitat protection over logging.

8. Grootbos Private Nature Reserve, South Africa

Focus: Fynbos and marine conservation

Grootbos protects 6,000 acres of critically endangered fynbos vegetation, one of the world's six floral kingdoms. The Grootbos Foundation operates marine conservation programs including whale and shark research, a horse rehabilitation center, and community development initiatives that employ and train 50+ local people. The Green Futures horticulture academy trains unemployed youth in sustainable agriculture and conservation landscaping.

9. Kasiiya Papagayo, Costa Rica

Focus: Tropical dry forest restoration

Kasiiya occupies a restored tropical dry forest that was once degraded cattle ranching land. The lodge has reforested hundreds of acres, creating habitat corridors that have attracted the return of howler monkeys, coatis, and over 200 bird species. Conservation programs include a sea turtle nesting protection program on nearby beaches and coral reef monitoring in the Gulf of Papagayo.

10. Misool Eco Resort, Raja Ampat, Indonesia

Focus: Marine conservation and no-take zone management

Misool established a 300,000-acre no-take marine protected area in Raja Ampat, one of the most biodiverse marine ecosystems on Earth. Since the resort began patrolling the area, shark populations have increased 25-fold, and manta ray populations have surged. The resort runs entirely on solar power and has pioneered coral restoration techniques. Revenue from diving guests directly funds ranger patrols that prevent illegal fishing.

11-15: Additional Conservation Leaders

11. Volcanoes Safaris, Uganda/Rwanda - Mountain gorilla conservation with community benefit sharing. Every trek booking funds gorilla veterinary care and anti-poaching patrols.

12. Three Camel Lodge, Mongolia - Snow leopard and wild camel conservation in the Gobi Desert. Community-owned model supports nomadic herder livelihoods.

13. Nimmo Bay Wilderness Resort, Canada - Grizzly bear and Pacific salmon conservation through habitat protection and research partnerships with First Nations communities.

14. Jean-Michel Cousteau Resort, Fiji - Coral reef conservation, marine biology education, and community marine protected area management across Savusavu Bay.

15. Hacienda AltaGracia, Costa Rica - Reforestation of former agricultural land, wildlife corridor creation, and community education programs in the Perez Zeledon valley.

How to Choose a Conservation Lodge

Ask these questions before booking: What percentage of revenue goes to conservation? What specific conservation outcomes has the lodge achieved? Does the lodge publish annual conservation impact reports? Are conservation programs run by qualified scientists or conservation professionals? Does the lodge partner with recognized conservation organizations? Are community members employed in and benefiting from conservation programs?

Genuine conservation lodges welcome these questions and answer them with data, not marketing language.

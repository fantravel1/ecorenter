Destination Intelligence

Not all "eco-friendly" destinations are created equal.

A country can have pristine beaches and still exploit its workers. A destination can boast protected areas while its resorts dump greywater. Our destination rankings look beyond marketing and measure what actually matters: how much of the economy runs on renewables, how much land is genuinely protected, how well tourism benefits local communities, and how well it supports the nomad lifestyle without environmental cost.

Destination Profiles

Why Each Destination Ranks Where It Does

#1 Costa Rica (92.4)

Decades of ecotourism investment pay off. 98% renewable grid, 25% protected land, 380+ verified eco-resorts, and strong community benefit frameworks. Weakest area: nomad infrastructure outside San Jose (improving with Starlink). The global benchmark for sustainable tourism policy.

#2 Kenya (84.4)

The community conservancy model is unmatched globally: over 160 conservancies protecting 11% of the country with tourism revenue flowing directly to communities. Highest community impact score (96). Weaker in grid renewables (72%) but individual lodges often operate 100% off-grid solar.

#3 Portugal (83.0)

Europe's leading sustainable travel destination. Best nomad infrastructure globally (94) with fiber internet, digital nomad visa, and extensive co-working. Growing eco-resort sector with strong EU environmental regulations. Protected areas expanding through rewilding initiatives.

#4 Bali (77.0)

Highest eco-resort density in the collection (94) with exceptional community scores (90) driven by traditional Balinese cooperative systems. Significantly held back by Indonesia's low renewable energy percentage (45%) and waste management challenges. Individual resorts often far exceed national averages.

#5 Colombia (78.4)

Mega-biodiversity meets emerging eco-tourism. Strong protected area coverage (82) and community impact (88) driven by post-conflict peace tourism and indigenous-led conservation. Rapidly improving infrastructure, though rural connectivity remains a challenge for nomads.

#6 Mexico (76.0)

High eco-resort density (86) with rich indigenous tourism traditions. Strong community impact through ejido (communal land) systems. Held back by lower renewable energy adoption (52%) and inconsistent environmental enforcement. Regions like Oaxaca and Chiapas outperform national averages significantly.

How We Score

Destination Scoring Criteria

Eco-Resort Density (20%)

Number of EcoRenter-verified eco-resorts relative to total tourism accommodation. Higher density indicates stronger industry commitment to sustainability. Adjusted for country size and tourism volume.

Renewable Energy (20%)

National grid renewable energy percentage combined with resort-level renewable adoption. Both matter: grid renewables reduce the carbon footprint of all tourism, while resort-level adoption shows industry leadership.

Protected Areas (20%)

Percentage of land and marine areas under legal protection, weighted by enforcement effectiveness. Includes national parks, community conservancies, marine reserves, and UNESCO sites with active management plans.

Nomad Infrastructure (20%)

Internet quality and availability, co-working space density, visa accessibility for remote workers, cost of living, safety, and healthcare access. Scored from traveler data and infrastructure measurements.

Community Impact (20%)

How well tourism revenue benefits local communities: local employment rates in tourism, wage fairness, community ownership of tourism assets, cultural preservation programs, and indigenous tourism development.

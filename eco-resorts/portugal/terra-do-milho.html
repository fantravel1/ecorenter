The Story

Building a Life From Straw and Sunlight

Terra do Milho sits in Portugal's Alentejo interior, an intentional community that has spent years building structures from straw bales and earth plaster, powered by sun, fed by permaculture gardens. The straw-bale buildings provide R-values three times higher than conventional walls. Finished with lime and earth plasters, the walls breathe naturally. In winter, a small wood stove heats efficiently. In summer, thick walls keep interiors cool without air conditioning.

The permaculture food forest has been developing for over a decade. Fig, olive, almond, pomegranate, and citrus form the canopy. Vegetable beds and herb spirals create multi-layered production. Composting toilets return nutrients to soil. The kitchen serves meals based almost entirely on what the land produces.

Energy: Off-Grid by Conviction

Entirely off-grid. Solar panels with battery storage generate all electricity. The Alentejo's 300+ sunny days make solar highly reliable. Cooking uses solar ovens, rocket stoves, and gas. Hot water from solar thermal tubes. Total energy per guest is a fraction of conventional hotels.

Community: Living the Questions

Residents share meals, work, and the questions of sustainable living. Guests join this exploration. No televisions, minimal WiFi. What there is: honest conversation, hands-on learning, spectacular stargazing, and the profound quiet of a life aligned with its values. Workshops in natural building, permaculture, and cooking are open to visitors and locals.

The Story

Where Waste Becomes Wealth and Coral Rebuilds Itself

Koh Kood is the fourth largest island in Thailand yet remains one of its least developed -- a place where waterfalls cascade through old-growth rainforest to empty white sand beaches, and fishing villages operate much as they have for generations. When Sonu Shivdasani chose this island for Soneva Kiri, the commitment was explicit: the resort would leave the island better than it found it. That promise has been tested and measured over years of operation, and the results -- documented through audited sustainability reports -- represent one of the most comprehensive eco-luxury models in Southeast Asia.

The SLOW LIFE philosophy (Sustainable, Local, Organic, Wellness, Learning, Inspiring, Fun, Experiences) is not a marketing tagline but an operational framework. No shoes, no news. Dining is farm-to-table from the organic gardens. Children learn about marine biology at The Den. Adults stargaze through a professional observatory. And beneath the surface, the Eco Centro waste facility and coral nursery do the invisible work that makes barefoot luxury genuinely sustainable.

Energy: Island Power Challenges

Operating on a remote island where grid electricity does not exist, Soneva Kiri generates its own power through a combination of solar arrays and efficient diesel generators. The solar installation provides approximately 30% of the resort's electricity, with ongoing expansion planned. Every villa is designed for passive cooling -- high ceilings, cross-ventilation, and mature tree canopy shading reduce air conditioning demand. Energy monitoring systems track consumption in real time across all facilities, and the resort publishes annual energy data in its sustainability report.

Water: Island Conservation

Fresh water on Koh Kood comes from wells and rainwater. Soneva Kiri operates a desalination system supplemented by extensive rainwater harvesting across the property. Greywater is treated on-site and reused for landscape irrigation. All pools use eco-friendly filtration. Water consumption is monitored per villa and reported annually. The resort funds community water infrastructure improvements in nearby villages, ensuring its presence does not compete with local water resources.

Waste: The Eco Centro Model

The Eco Centro is Soneva Kiri's centerpiece sustainability achievement. This on-site waste management facility processes over 90% of the resort's waste stream. Glass is crushed and recycled into construction materials. Organic waste is composted or converted to biochar for the farm. Plastics collected from the island and surrounding ocean are sorted and sent for recycling. The facility employs local staff and doubles as an education center where guests witness the full waste lifecycle. The Eco Centro model has been replicated at other Soneva properties and inspired waste management improvements across Koh Kood.

Community: Fishing Village Partnership

Soneva Kiri employs a significant percentage of its workforce from Koh Kood's fishing villages, providing training, healthcare, and above-market wages. The resort purchases fish from local sustainable fishing cooperatives, supporting traditional livelihoods. Education programs fund scholarships for island children. Infrastructure contributions include water systems, waste management support, and healthcare access. The Soneva Foundation channels a portion of room revenue into community projects across Koh Kood and mainland Trat province.

Biodiversity: Coral Nurseries and Forest Protection

The coral nursery program cultivates resilient coral fragments on underwater structures before transplanting them to degraded reef sites around the island. Over 2,000 coral colonies have been established across multiple restoration sites, monitored quarterly by marine biologists. Guests participate in coral planting dives and reef surveys. On land, the resort's 100-acre property preserves old-growth tropical forest, with marked nature trails and a resident naturalist who documents wildlife including hornbills, monitor lizards, and sea eagles. Night walks reveal fireflies, cicadas, and nocturnal wildlife.

Honest Assessment

Trade-offs and Limitations

No resort is perfect. Here is what we document alongside the strengths.

Private Flights: The scenic private propeller plane from Bangkok, while convenient, adds a per-guest carbon footprint that partially offsets the resort's on-site environmental gains. Soneva operates a mandatory carbon levy to offset travel emissions, but offsetting remains a contested climate strategy.

Diesel Generation: Despite growing solar capacity, the resort still relies on diesel generators for the majority of its electricity. Island energy infrastructure is challenging, but the fossil fuel dependency is a real limitation on the resort's carbon performance.

Ultra-Luxury Exclusivity: Soneva Kiri's nightly rates place it among the most expensive resorts in Asia. While the sustainability model is genuine and well-documented, it operates within an ultra-luxury economic tier that limits broader applicability and accessibility.

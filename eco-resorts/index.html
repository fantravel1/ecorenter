Our Verification Process

How We Separate Genuine Sustainability From Greenwashing

Every eco-resort in this database has been evaluated through EcoRenter's evidence-based verification system. We assess five sustainability dimensions -- energy, water, waste, community impact, and biodiversity -- using only verifiable data. Resorts are assigned an evidence tier (A, B, or C) based on the strength of their documentation, and an impact score based on measured outcomes. Tier D resorts -- those that fail verification or refuse transparency -- are never published.

No resort can pay for placement, improved rankings, or favorable assessment. Our independence from booking revenue ensures every listing reflects truth, not marketing budgets. We also document trade-offs honestly: a resort may excel in renewable energy but lag in waste management, and we will tell you both.

Common Questions

Eco-Resorts: Frequently Asked Questions

EcoRenter uses a four-tier evidence system. Tier A resorts hold third-party certifications from recognized bodies like GSTC, Rainforest Alliance, or Green Globe and have undergone independent audits. Tier B resorts publish transparent operational documentation including energy consumption, water usage, waste metrics, and community payments. Tier C resorts are verified through traveler and NGO reports. Tier D resorts are never published. Every listing includes impact scores across energy, water, waste, community, and biodiversity.

Tier A eco-resorts have been certified by recognized third-party organizations and have undergone independent sustainability audits. This is the gold standard of verification. Tier B resorts publish transparent operational data -- energy bills, water reports, waste metrics, community payments -- but may not yet hold formal certifications. Both tiers represent genuine commitment to sustainability; the difference lies in the type and formality of evidence provided.

EcoRenter's database covers 12 countries: Costa Rica, Portugal, Bali (Indonesia), Mexico, Kenya, Colombia, Thailand, Tanzania, Ecuador, New Zealand, Iceland, and Rwanda. These destinations were selected for their high concentration of verified eco-resorts, strong sustainability documentation, and infrastructure that supports responsible travelers and digital nomads.

A 'High Impact' designation means the eco-resort scores above 80 out of 100 across all five sustainability dimensions: energy (renewable sources and efficiency), water (conservation and treatment), waste (reduction and recycling), community (local employment and fair wages), and biodiversity (habitat protection and restoration). These scores are based on verified evidence, not self-reported marketing claims.

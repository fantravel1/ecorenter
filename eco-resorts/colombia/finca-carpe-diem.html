The Story

Where Coffee Grows Under Bird Song

Finca Carpe Diem sits in the hills above Salento, Quindio, in the heart of Colombia's UNESCO-recognized Coffee Cultural Landscape. The farm practices agroecology -- a farming philosophy that works with natural systems rather than against them. Coffee grows under a diverse canopy of native and fruit trees that provide habitat for over 200 bird species, including rare endemics found nowhere else on Earth.

The farm operates as both accommodation and working agricultural demonstration. Guests participate in coffee harvesting, learn about organic processing, and can join bird watching walks led by local guides who know every species by song. Community farming programs teach neighboring smallholders agroecological techniques, strengthening local food sovereignty.

Agroecology in Practice

Unlike monoculture coffee production, Finca Carpe Diem's shade-grown agroforestry system integrates multiple crops: coffee under native tree canopy, interspersed with fruit trees (plantain, citrus, avocado), medicinal herbs, and vegetable gardens. This diversity creates ecological resilience -- natural pest control, soil building, water retention -- while producing food beyond coffee. Composting and vermiculture replace chemical fertilizers. The result is soil that improves each year.

Community Impact

The farm employs local families and shares agroecological knowledge through regular training workshops. Neighboring smallholders receive seedlings, compost, and technical support. A cooperative marketing program helps small coffee producers access specialty markets at premium prices. Cultural programming preserves traditional Colombian rural practices and music.

Honest Assessment

Trade-offs and Limitations

Basic Accommodation: A working farm, not a luxury resort. Expect simple rooms and shared facilities.

Limited Renewable Energy: Grid-dependent for electricity. Solar installation is planned but not yet implemented.

Access Challenges: Rural location requires car or arranged transport from Salento.

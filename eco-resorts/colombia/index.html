Why Colombia

The Biodiversity Superpower Awakens

Colombia harbors more bird species than any other country, more orchid species, and the second-highest overall biodiversity on Earth. For decades, internal conflict limited tourism development. Now, as peace takes root, a new generation of eco-entrepreneurs is building sustainable tourism that protects rather than exploits this extraordinary natural heritage. The Coffee Triangle's agroforestry systems, the Amazon's indigenous-led conservation, and the emerging natural building movement all represent Colombia's potential as a sustainable travel destination of global significance.

Colombia Eco-Resorts FAQ

Colombia has undergone tremendous transformation. The regions covered by these verified eco-resorts -- Coffee Triangle, Leticia/Amazon, Quindio -- are well-established tourism areas with good infrastructure and security. Standard travel precautions apply.

Second most biodiverse country globally. Most bird species of any nation (1,900+). More orchid species than anywhere. Unique ecosystems from Andes to Amazon to Caribbean.

The Eje Cafetero (Coffee Triangle) is a UNESCO Cultural Landscape comprising the departments of Caldas, Quindio, and Risaralda. Traditional coffee farming under shade canopy creates habitat for hundreds of bird species.

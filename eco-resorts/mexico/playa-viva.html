The Story

Regeneration, Not Just Sustainability

Playa Viva does not aim to minimize its impact -- it aims to be a net positive force on the landscape. Founded on degraded former agricultural land on Mexico's Pacific coast, the resort is literally regenerating the ecosystem: planting native forest, restoring estuary function, protecting endangered sea turtles, and building a permaculture food system that improves soil health year over year.

The treehouse suites, elevated in the recovering forest canopy, are icons of the regenerative approach. Built from local materials -- bamboo, palm thatch, and sustainably harvested hardwood -- they let guests sleep surrounded by the sounds of a landscape coming back to life. Below, the permaculture farm produces food that feeds both guests and the local community. The turtle sanctuary has released over 100,000 hatchlings since its founding.

Turtle Sanctuary

The sanctuary protects olive ridley and leatherback sea turtles during nesting season. Trained guards patrol the beach nightly, relocating nests to protected hatcheries when necessary. Guests can participate in hatchling releases -- one of conservation's most powerful educational experiences. Over 100,000 hatchlings released.

Permaculture and Community

The 200-acre property includes extensive permaculture food production. Surplus feeds the local community through a weekly farmers' market. Over 85% of staff are from surrounding villages. The Playa Viva Foundation funds school construction, healthcare access, and environmental education. Estuary restoration projects have returned critical wetland habitat.

Energy: Solar and Smart Design

Comprehensive solar array provides the majority of electricity. Treehouse design maximizes natural ventilation, eliminating air conditioning. Water heating is solar thermal. The resort targets complete energy independence through expanding solar capacity and battery storage.

Honest Assessment

Trade-offs and Limitations

Remote Access: The Pacific coast location requires significant travel from major airports, adding transportation emissions.

Seasonal Limitations: Tropical climate means intense heat and humidity in summer months. Hurricane season (June-November) affects operations.

Luxury Pricing: Treehouse suites command premium rates that limit accessibility.

The Story

Eight Decades of Organic Living

Founded in 1940, Rancho La Puerta is one of the oldest wellness resorts in the world. Long before organic agriculture was trendy, founders Edmond and Deborah Szekely were growing their own food without chemicals on a ranch near Tecate, Baja California. Today, the 3,000-acre property includes an organic farm, fitness facilities, spa, and sprawling gardens -- all still guided by the founding philosophy that human health and land health are inseparable.

The organic farm, La Cocina Que Canta, produces over 60% of the resort's food needs. Six acres of intensive organic production yield an extraordinary diversity: over 150 varieties of fruits, vegetables, and herbs. The remaining property includes native chaparral, oak woodland, and wildflower meadows that support local wildlife. Staff are primarily from Tecate and surrounding communities, many with multi-generational connections to the ranch.

The Farm: Eight Decades Without Chemicals

La Cocina Que Canta has never used synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers -- 80+ years of organic soil management. Composting, crop rotation, cover cropping, and integrated pest management maintain soil health. The result is soil carbon levels that exceed most agricultural land by orders of magnitude. The farm operates a seed-saving program, preserving heirloom varieties. Guests participate in farm tours, cooking classes, and harvest experiences.

Wellness and Community

The wellness programs integrate fitness, nutrition, mindfulness, and nature connection. Multiple decades of practice have refined the approach. Staff live and work in a community that reflects the values -- fair wages, health programs, housing support, and educational opportunities. The ranch's influence on Tecate's economy is significant, providing stable employment in a border region with limited opportunities.

Honest Assessment

Trade-offs and Limitations

Incomplete Energy Transition: Despite Baja California's excellent solar potential, the ranch has not yet achieved full renewable energy. Grid and generator use continues.

Water Scarcity: Baja California faces chronic water stress. While the ranch conserves carefully, the regional water situation is precarious.

No Formal Eco-Certification: Despite 80+ years of organic practice, the ranch has not pursued formal eco-tourism certification beyond USDA organic for the farm.

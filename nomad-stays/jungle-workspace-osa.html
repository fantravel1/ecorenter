Jungle Workspace Osa Peninsula: Treehouse Offices in the Most Biodiverse Place on Earth

Write code from a treehouse where scarlet macaws perch outside your window and humpback whales breach in the bay below. Solar-powered, Starlink-connected, and surrounded by more species per square meter than anywhere else on the planet. Monthly stays from $850 on Costa Rica's wild Pacific coast.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. Primary rainforest on Costa Rica's Osa Peninsula, the most biologically intense place on Earth.

The Space

Your office is in the canopy. Your colleagues are macaws.

"I was reviewing a pull request when a family of spider monkeys swung past my treehouse desk, close enough to count their fingers. My speed test showed 78Mbps. My heart rate was through the roof. There is no co-working space on Earth like this. The Osa changes what you think is possible for a workspace."

— Carlos, Backend Developer, 3-month resident

The Osa Peninsula holds 2.5% of the world's biodiversity in just 0.001% of its surface area. National Geographic called it "the most biologically intense place on Earth." The Jungle Workspace has built three treehouse offices 6-8 meters above the forest floor, connected by suspended walkways, each powered by a 20kW solar array with battery storage and connected to the world through Starlink. Below, the primary rainforest teems with four species of monkeys, tapirs, jaguars, and over 460 bird species. The Pacific Ocean, visible from the canopy, hosts migrating humpback whales for eight months of the year.

Treehouse Offices

Three elevated platforms built into massive ceiba and strangler fig trees, each seating 4-6 nomads. Local hardwood construction with minimal forest impact. Open sides with retractable rain screens provide canopy-level immersion. Monkeys, toucans, and macaws pass at eye level. Each treehouse has dedicated desks, power, WiFi, and panoramic rainforest views.

Ground Pavilion

A thatched-roof pavilion at ground level offers 8 additional workstations for those who prefer solid ground or need video call stability. Climate-controlled with ceiling fans and mist systems. Sound-insulated phone booth. Meeting table for 8. This is the recommended space for important client calls and presentations.

Jungle Cabins

Eight private cabins on stilts with ensuite bathrooms, screened windows for jungle airflow, ceiling fans, and private decks. Each cabin is positioned for maximum privacy and wildlife viewing. Solar-heated water. USB charging stations. Mosquito-netted beds. The sound of howler monkeys serves as your morning alarm.

Wildlife Experiences

Whale watching excursions (seasonal), mangrove kayaking through crocodile habitat, guided night hikes with headlamps, Corcovado National Park day trips, snorkeling at Cano Island, and sport fishing. The property's 50-acre private reserve connects to Corcovado through a wildlife corridor that hosts jaguars, pumas, and all four monkey species.

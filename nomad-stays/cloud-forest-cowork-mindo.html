Cloud Forest Co-Work Mindo: Your Office Among Hummingbirds

Write code while 20 species of hummingbirds flash outside your window. Take afternoon breaks on chocolate farm tours. Hike to waterfalls after your last call. Mindo's cloud forest delivers 80Mbps and a cost of living that lets you save money while living better. Monthly stays from $350.

The Space

Where hummingbirds are your coworkers and chocolate is your break room.

"I came for a month and stayed four. The internet works, the coffee is incredible, and every morning I watch hummingbirds while my code compiles. When the afternoon clouds roll in, I close my laptop and hike to a waterfall. I have never been more productive or more content."

— Sarah, full-stack developer from Portland

The Cloud Forest Co-Work occupies a converted farmhouse on the edge of Mindo village, surrounded by cloud forest on three sides. The workspace is a covered open-air pavilion with views into the canopy where hummingbird feeders attract a constant aerial show. Six dedicated desks with ergonomic chairs and Ethernet drops line the interior wall. Additional open seating with beanbags and hammock chairs fills the garden side. The orchid garden contains over 200 species and doubles as a contemplation space for thinking through difficult problems. A community kitchen serves breakfast from local ingredients and is available for self-catering throughout the day.

Covered Pavilion Workspace

Open-air covered workspace with 12 workstations, ergonomic chairs, and power at every seat. Six dedicated desks with Ethernet drops for maximum stability. Canopy views and hummingbird feeders within sight. Natural ventilation keeps the space comfortable at Mindo's 1,200-meter elevation. No air conditioning needed year-round.

Garden Work Zones

Hammock chairs and beanbag seating scattered through the orchid garden for casual work sessions. A covered gazebo provides a private meeting space for calls. Each zone has WiFi coverage and nearby power outlets. The garden work areas are particularly popular during clear mornings when the hummingbird activity peaks.

Community Kitchen

Full kitchen with local coffee, filtered water, and basic provisions. Breakfast served daily from local ingredients. Self-catering available for lunch and dinner. Weekly community dinners bring all residents together. Mindo village is a 10-minute walk for restaurants and markets.

Starlink Backup

A Starlink satellite system provides automatic failover during rare fiber outages. The backup activates within seconds and maintains approximately 50Mbps download during fiber downtime. Combined with the primary connection, this provides 98.5% quarterly uptime suitable for consistent remote work schedules.

After-Work Activities

What to Do When the Laptop Closes

Chocolate Farm Tours

Mindo is surrounded by artisanal chocolate farms where cacao is grown, fermented, roasted, and processed into bars. Weekend tours with partner farms show the complete bean-to-bar process. You taste cacao at every stage and make your own bar to take home. These are working agricultural operations producing chocolate sold locally and internationally -- not tourist attractions. A perfect afternoon break from screen time.

Orchid Gardens

The on-site garden holds over 200 orchid species, many found only in the Choco cloud forest. Walking the garden paths between work sessions provides a reset that no coffee break can match. The diversity of forms -- from microscopic blooms to dinner-plate-sized flowers -- demonstrates the evolutionary creativity that makes this ecosystem globally significant. Staff maintain species identification labels for self-guided exploration.

Waterfall Hikes

Mindo's river valley contains over 20 waterfalls accessible by hiking trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging scrambles. One guided waterfall hike per week is included in the monthly rate. Additional hikes can be arranged with local guides. The Nambillo River trail passes seven waterfalls in a single three-hour loop. Natural swimming pools at waterfall bases provide refreshment during warm-season hikes. All trails are within 30 minutes of the workspace.

Hummingbird Photography

With 15-20 species visiting the workspace feeders, Mindo is a photographer's paradise. Early morning and late afternoon light creates ideal conditions. The resident Green-crowned Brilliant, Violet-tailed Sylph, and Booted Racket-tail are particularly photogenic. No telephoto lens needed -- feeders are positioned within three meters of seating areas. Several nomads have built successful photography side projects from their Mindo stay.

Rice Terrace Retreat Sidemen: Traditional Balinese Living with Modern Connectivity

Live in a traditional Balinese compound where rice terraces cascade to the river below and Mount Agung towers above. Morning yoga overlooking emerald paddies, organic Balinese breakfast, and 100Mbps fiber for the workday ahead. Sidemen offers authentic Bali at $400 per month, before the crowds found it.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The iconic rice terraces of Sidemen valley in East Bali.

The Space

This is the Bali that existed before Instagram.

"I came to Sidemen because it was cheap. I stayed because it was real. The family next door invited me to their temple ceremony. The rice farmers wave every morning. The yoga teacher lives in the village. The WiFi is fast enough for video calls. And the rent is less than my gym membership in Sydney. I found the Bali everyone says no longer exists."

— Sophie, Freelance Writer, 6-month resident

The Sidemen valley in East Bali is what many travelers imagine when they dream of Bali: terraced rice paddies descending steep hillsides, the volcanic cone of Mount Agung rising above, and village life centered on temples, ceremonies, and the ancient subak irrigation system. The Rice Terrace Retreat occupies a traditional compound belonging to a Balinese family who converted it for nomad residents while preserving its cultural authenticity. The result is a co-living space that immerses you in genuine Balinese village life rather than the tourist bubble of southern Bali.

Main House Workspace

The compound's main house has been converted into a co-working space with 8 dedicated desks, ergonomic chairs, and monitor stands. Traditional carved wooden walls and a high thatched ceiling create an atmospheric work environment. Views through open windows look across rice terraces to Mount Agung. Two phone corners for private calls.

Garden Pavilion

A traditional bale (open pavilion) in the garden provides an outdoor workspace with 4 low tables and floor cushions on raised bamboo platforms. Shaded by frangipani trees. Power outlets and WiFi throughout. The preferred workspace for morning hours before the midday heat, and again in the late afternoon as the light turns golden over the terraces.

Yoga Studio

The former rice granary has been converted into an open-sided yoga studio with hardwood floors, facing east toward Mount Agung and the sunrise. Daily morning yoga at 7am, various styles. Meditation garden with lotus pond adjacent. Available for personal practice throughout the day. Quarterly teacher training intensives for longer-stay residents.

Traditional Rooms

Private rooms in individual pavilions surrounding the central courtyard. Traditional Balinese architecture with modern comforts: proper mattresses, ceiling fans, mosquito nets, private terraces. Shared bathrooms with solar-heated water. The compound's family temple is visible from most rooms, and daily offerings create a gentle rhythm of incense and flower petals.

Cenote Cowork Valladolid: Colonial Hacienda Living with 50 Cenotes at Your Doorstep

Work in a restored 19th-century hacienda where thick limestone walls keep you cool, 200Mbps fiber keeps you connected, and 50 cenotes within cycling distance keep your afternoons extraordinary. Authentic Yucatecan town life, Mayan cultural immersion, and monthly stays from $600.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The colorful colonial streets of Valladolid, gateway to the Yucatan's cenotes.

The Space

Limestone walls. Fiber internet. Underground rivers.

"I found my rhythm in Valladolid: morning work under the mango tree in the courtyard, afternoon swim in a different cenote every day, evening tacos at the family restaurant around the corner for two dollars. The hacienda has 200Mbps. The town has soul. And the cenotes are the most beautiful places I have ever swum. This is the Mexico that package tourism will never show you."

— Miguel, Graphic Designer, 5-month resident

Valladolid is a colonial gem of pastel buildings, Mayan heritage, and subterranean waterways that few international travelers discover. The Cenote Cowork occupies a restored hacienda on the town's quiet northern edge, where the garden backs onto open countryside and a cenote is a 5-minute bicycle ride away. The restoration preserved 150-year-old limestone walls, hand-painted Mayan tile floors, and arched colonial doorways while adding 200Mbps fiber, solar water heating, and an organic garden in the former sisal fields. It is a place where history, culture, and remote work infrastructure coexist without compromise.

Courtyard Workspace

The central courtyard, shaded by mango and orange trees with a colonial fountain, serves as the main workspace. 12 desks with comfortable seating arranged under the tree canopy. Power outlets at every station. The thick hacienda walls provide natural sound insulation from the street. A covered colonnade offers rain-protected seating during the brief summer downpours.

Library Room

The hacienda's former salon has been converted into a quiet work room with 6 dedicated desks, high ceilings with exposed wooden beams, and original tile floors. Air-conditioned for Yucatan's hottest months. Bookshelves hold literature on Mayan history, Mexican cooking, and Yucatecan culture. Two phone corners for private calls.

Hacienda Rooms

Private rooms with original tile floors, thick limestone walls keeping interiors cool, high ceilings with wooden beams, and colonial-style furniture. Ensuite bathrooms with solar-heated water. Ceiling fans and air conditioning. Each room opens onto the courtyard or garden. The walls are so thick that room-to-room noise is virtually zero.

Organic Garden and Kitchen

The former sisal fields now host an organic garden producing herbs, chiles, citrus, and tropical fruit. A traditional outdoor kitchen hosts weekly Yucatecan cooking classes: cochinita pibil, papadzules, sopa de lima, and panuchos. Organic breakfast from the garden is included daily. The kitchen also hosts community dinners every Wednesday evening.

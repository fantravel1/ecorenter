Cloud Forest Hub Monteverde: Work Powered by Waterfalls Among 500 Bird Species

Code in a cloud forest where your electricity comes from a waterfall, your lunch grows in the greenhouse next door, and resplendent quetzals perch in the trees outside your window. 100Mbps fiber, micro-hydro power, and the richest biodiversity per square meter on the continent. Monthly stays from $700.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The cloud forest canopy surrounding the Monteverde co-working hub.

The Space

Your power bill is a waterfall. Your office mates are quetzals.

"I set up my laptop next to the greenhouse where my salad was growing. A hummingbird hovered at the feeder two meters from my screen. The power came from a stream I could hear from my desk. I had 95Mbps on the speed test. I thought: this is the most advanced office I have ever worked in, and it was built in a cloud forest."

— Ana, Full-Stack Developer, 6-month resident

Monteverde's cloud forest is one of the most biodiverse ecosystems on Earth, home to 500 bird species, 120 mammal species, and thousands of plant species including over 500 orchids. The Cloud Forest Hub sits at 1,400 meters on the forest edge, drawing 100% of its electricity from a micro-hydro turbine powered by a mountain stream. An organic greenhouse provides fresh produce year-round. A bird observatory with professional optics offers world-class birdwatching from the comfort of your morning coffee break.

Main Workspace

A timber-framed building with floor-to-ceiling windows facing the cloud forest canopy. 12 dedicated desks with ergonomic chairs, adjustable monitors, and task lighting. Natural light floods the space through the forest-facing glass wall. Two phone booths and a 6-person meeting room with video conferencing. Heated during cool cloud forest mornings.

Greenhouse Lounge

Adjacent to the organic greenhouse, a glass-roofed lounge provides an alternative work environment surrounded by growing plants. Casual seating with laptop-height tables among tomato vines, herb walls, and tropical fruit trees. The warmest space in the compound, naturally heated by the greenhouse effect. WiFi and power throughout.

Bird Observatory

An elevated platform with a weatherproof roof, Swarovski spotting scopes, and field guides doubles as a unique work spot for those who can handle the distraction of quetzals and toucans. Hummingbird feeders attract 12 species daily. A whiteboard tracks species sightings. WiFi and power available at two observatory desks.

Micro-Hydro System

A Pelton wheel turbine generates 15kW continuously from a 40-meter head stream. 20kWh battery bank handles peak loads. Zero carbon, zero stream impact (water returned below turbine). Solar backup for rare dry-season low-flow. The hub has never purchased grid electricity since the turbine was installed in 2020.

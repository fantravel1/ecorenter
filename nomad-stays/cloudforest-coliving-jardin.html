Cloud Forest Coliving Jardin: Colombia's Most Beautiful Village, Your Most Affordable Office

Work from a mountain eco-house in a painted colonial village where coffee costs fifty cents, the Andean cock-of-the-rock displays at dawn, and natural hot springs are a bicycle ride away. 80Mbps fiber, organic meals, and monthly stays from just $450 in one of South America's hidden gems.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The lush mountain landscape surrounding Jardin, Antioquia, Colombia.

The Space

A fifty-cent coffee. A million-dollar view. An eighty-megabit connection.

"Jardin ruined me for other places. I wake up to cloud forest mist, work on the veranda with views across a valley of coffee and banana plants, eat the best bandeja paisa of my life for three dollars, and end the day in natural hot springs. The WiFi is 75Mbps. My rent is $450. I have been here eight months and I am not leaving."

— Laura, Freelance Copywriter, 8-month resident

Jardin (meaning "garden" in Spanish) is a perfectly preserved Antioqueno pueblo of 14,000 people nestled at 1,750 meters in the western Andes. No international chain stores. No tourist buses. Just painted colonial buildings, a central plaza where farmers drink tinto and play dominoes, and surrounding cloud forests that host some of the richest birdlife on the continent. The Cloud Forest Coliving is a renovated eco-house on the town's edge, where the garden merges with coffee fields and the cloud forest rises above. It proves that the world's most beautiful office can also be the most affordable.

Veranda Workspace

The house's covered veranda wraps around two sides, providing the main workspace with panoramic views across the Jardin valley. 8 dedicated desks with comfortable chairs, power strips, and USB charging. The veranda is weather-protected by the deep eaves typical of Antioqueno architecture. Mountain breezes keep temperatures comfortable year-round without fans or air conditioning.

Indoor Study

A quiet room with 4 dedicated desks for focused work and video calls. Original tile floors, whitewashed walls, and high ceilings. The only air-conditioned space, though Jardin's climate rarely requires it. Bookshelves hold literature on Colombian culture, cloud forest ecology, and coffee production. A small meeting area with whiteboard seats 6.

Private Rooms

Eight private rooms with mountain or garden views. Handmade wooden furniture, comfortable beds, warm blankets for cool Andean nights, and private balconies or garden access. Shared bathrooms with solar-heated water. The house is built from local wood and adobe with traditional Antioqueno architectural details preserved throughout.

Experiences

Weekly coffee farm visits with cupping sessions. Guided birdwatching walks to the Andean cock-of-the-rock reserve. Monthly hot springs excursions. Cloud forest hikes through orchid-laden trails. Caving trips to the Cueva del Esplendor waterfall cave. Trout fishing in mountain streams. Plaza evenings with locals. Each experience connects you to the landscape and community that make Jardin extraordinary.

Energy and Materials

Solar water heating provides 80% of hot water. Colombia's grid is 70% hydroelectric, making even grid electricity largely renewable. The house was renovated using traditional adobe and local wood construction techniques with modern insulation. LED lighting throughout. Natural ventilation eliminates the need for air conditioning at 1,750 meters elevation.

Food and Community

96% of food sourced within 20 kilometers: coffee from neighboring fincas, vegetables from the Jardin market, trout from local streams, fruit from the garden. All 6 staff are Jardin residents paid above local wages. 4% of revenue supports the town's cloud forest conservation trust. The coliving participates in annual community tree planting days, adding 200 native trees annually to degraded cloud forest margins.

Highland Hub Cuenca: Work From a UNESCO World Heritage City

Your desk overlooks colonial cathedrals and cobblestone streets in one of South America's most livable cities. 120Mbps fiber keeps you connected while $2 market lunches keep you fed. Weekend Panama hat workshops and Cajas National Park hikes remind you that productivity is not the only measure of a life well-lived. Monthly stays from $300.

The Space

A restored colonial house where cathedral bells mark your Pomodoro intervals.

"Cuenca is the best-kept secret in the nomad world. The internet is fast, the weather is perfect, the food is incredible, and everything costs a fraction of what I paid in Lisbon or Bali. I spend my mornings coding from a rooftop with cathedral views and my afternoons exploring markets where the vendors know my name. At $300 a month, I am saving more money than I ever have while living better than I ever have."

— Marcus, product designer from Berlin

The Highland Hub occupies a beautifully restored colonial house in Cuenca's UNESCO-listed historic center. The ground floor houses the main co-working space with 16 dedicated desks, meeting room, and phone booths. The rooftop terrace provides an open-air workspace with panoramic views of Cuenca's blue-domed cathedrals and surrounding mountains. Private rooms are upstairs in the same building -- your commute is walking downstairs. A community kitchen serves breakfast from ingredients sourced at the morning market. The location is walkable to everything: markets, restaurants, laundry, pharmacies, and the river promenade where Cuencanos jog at sunset.

After-Work Activities

What to Do When the Laptop Closes

Panama Hat Workshops

The so-called Panama hat is actually Ecuadorian, and Cuenca is its capital. Master weavers in surrounding villages create superfino hats from toquilla straw that take up to six months to complete. The weaving is so fine that the best hats can hold water. Weekend workshop visits allow nomads to try basic weaving techniques and meet artisans keeping this UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage craft alive. Purchasing directly from weavers supports livelihoods threatened by factory imitations.

Cajas National Park

Just 45 minutes from the workspace, Cajas National Park protects 28,544 hectares of paramo grassland studded with over 230 glacial lakes. Hikes range from easy lakeside loops to challenging high-altitude traverses reaching 4,450 meters. The park is Cuenca's primary water source, making its conservation directly relevant to city sustainability. Monthly group trips are included in the stay. Condors soar above the paramo. Polylepis forests at the treeline are among the highest-elevation woodlands in the world.

Local Market Immersion

Cuenca's markets are living institutions. Weekly guided visits introduce vendors by name, explain seasonal produce cycles, demonstrate ingredient selection, and teach Ecuadorian cooking techniques. Mercado 10 de Agosto serves $2 almuerzo lunches. Mercado 9 de Octubre overflows with fruits, vegetables, and herbs. The flower market on the main plaza fills the morning air with fragrance. These visits build real relationships with local vendors and give nomads the knowledge to cook authentic meals that respect local food culture.

Colonial City Exploration

Cuenca's UNESCO World Heritage historic center is walkable from the workspace. Blue-domed cathedrals, terracotta-roofed colonial houses, cobblestone streets, and river promenades create a living museum. Weekend city walking tours included in the stay cover architecture, history, and the stories behind the facades. The Pumapungo archaeological site preserves Inca ruins within the city. Art galleries, craft workshops, and traditional apothecaries line the streets. Cuenca rewards exploration on foot at a pace that recharges rather than exhausts.

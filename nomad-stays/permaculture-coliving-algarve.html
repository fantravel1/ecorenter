Permaculture Coliving Algarve: Off-Grid Community Where Your Food Grows While You Code

Live in an off-grid community powered by 300 days of Portuguese sunshine, eating from a food forest that produces year-round without irrigation, and connecting to the world through 150Mbps Starlink. The Algarve coast is 30 minutes away. The future of sustainable living is here, and it has fast WiFi. Monthly stays from €650.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The sun-drenched Algarve countryside surrounding the permaculture community.

The Space

The food grows itself. The sun powers everything. You just need WiFi.

"I arrived thinking off-grid meant sacrifice. I left understanding it means abundance. The food forest produces more than we can eat. The solar generates more than we consume. The Starlink is faster than my London flat. And the community dinners under the fig trees are the happiest meals I have ever had. This is not alternative living. This is intelligent living."

— James, UX Designer, 4-month resident

Set on 10 acres of former agricultural land in the Algarve hinterland, the Permaculture Coliving has spent 6 years transforming degraded farmland into a thriving food forest, solar-powered community, and digital nomad workspace. A 25kW solar array with 50kWh battery storage provides 100% of electricity. A 3-acre food forest produces 70% of communal meals. Rainwater harvesting and greywater recycling eliminate mains water dependency. And 150Mbps Starlink delivers the connectivity that makes remote work not just possible but pleasurable.

Main Workspace

A converted stone barn with thick walls keeping the interior cool in summer and warm in winter. 10 dedicated desks with ergonomic chairs, monitor stands, and USB-C power. Three skylights provide abundant natural light. Cork board walls for project planning. Two phone booths for private calls. The thick stone walls provide natural sound insulation.

Garden Workspace

An outdoor workspace under a grape arbor with 6 table stations, power outlets, and full WiFi coverage. Shaded by grapevines in summer, open to warming sun in winter. Views across the food forest to distant hills. The most popular workspace from April through October when the Algarve's climate is at its finest.

Accommodation

Choose between private cabins built from reclaimed materials or traditional yurts. All have proper beds, storage, lighting, and power. Shared bathrooms with solar-heated water. The yurts are surprisingly comfortable year-round with proper insulation and ventilation. Private ensuite cabins available at a premium rate.

Community Facilities

Communal kitchen for those who enjoy cooking. Outdoor dining area under ancient fig trees. Natural swimming pool with plant-based filtration. Yoga and meditation platform. Workshop space for permaculture courses. Library with books on sustainability, design, and philosophy. Bicycles for exploring the Algarve countryside.

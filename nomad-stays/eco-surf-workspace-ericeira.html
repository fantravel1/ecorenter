Eco Surf Workspace Ericeira: Ride Waves, Ship Code, Save the Ocean

Work from Europe's only World Surfing Reserve in a solar-powered co-living that takes ocean conservation as seriously as wave quality. 250Mbps fiber, included surf lessons, weekly beach cleanups, and a community of surfers who code. Monthly stays from €800.

The Vibe

The best meetings happen in the lineup.

"I surf at dawn, work until sunset, clean the beach on Wednesdays, and do my best creative thinking paddling out to the break. My code is better because the ocean clears my mind. My ocean is cleaner because my co-living teaches me to care. That is the Ericeira loop."

— Marcus, Software Engineer, 6-month resident

Ericeira is a fishing village 35 minutes north of Lisbon that has become Europe's surf capital. Its seven world-class breaks attract surfers from around the globe. The Eco Surf Workspace channels that energy into a community of ocean-loving remote workers who balance productive work days with dawn patrol sessions, conservation activities, and the laid-back rhythm of a Portuguese coastal village. Solar panels power your laptop. The waves power your creativity.

Ocean-View Workspace

The co-working space occupies the top floor with panoramic ocean views. You can literally check the surf conditions from your desk. 16 dedicated workstations with ergonomic chairs, monitor arms, and standing desk options. Two phone booths. One meeting room. A communal area with bean bags for casual brainstorming. Surf check cameras displayed on a lobby screen.

Surf Facilities

Secure board storage for up to 30 boards. Wetsuit drying racks and outdoor shower. Equipment rental for residents without their own gear: sustainably made boards from local shapers. Surf forecast posted daily. Four included group lessons per month with certified instructors. The closest break is a 3-minute walk.

Ocean Conservation

Weekly beach cleanups every Wednesday afternoon (residents are expected to participate at least twice monthly). Monthly ocean health monitoring with a marine biology research partner. Reef-safe sunscreen provided in common areas. Educational film nights about ocean conservation. 3% of monthly fees fund the Ericeira Marine Protected Area campaign.

Sustainable Living

12kW rooftop solar array. Electric bike fleet for village transportation. Zero single-use plastics. Communal kitchen sourcing from local organic farms and the Ericeira fish market. Composting system. Rainwater collection for garden irrigation. Reclaimed wood furniture built by a local carpenter from old fishing boats. The building is as much a conservation project as a workspace.

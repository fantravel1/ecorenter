Selva Digital Retreat: Code in the Canopy, Powered by Sun and Water

Work from the heart of Costa Rica's most biodiverse peninsula. Your desk overlooks a wildlife corridor where monkeys commute through the treetops. Solar panels and a micro-hydro turbine power your laptop. The organic farm feeds your body. The jungle feeds your soul. Monthly from $750.

The Vision

Off-grid does not mean offline. It means off the grid that destroys.

"People ask how I can run a tech workspace in the jungle. I tell them: the jungle has been running complex systems for 50 million years without a single server crash. We just tapped into its energy and added WiFi. The monkeys do not seem to mind."

— Diego Herrera, Founder, Selva Digital Retreat

The Selva Digital Retreat proves that the most inspiring workspace does not need to be in a glass tower. Built on the edge of Costa Rica's Osa Peninsula, one of the most biodiverse places on Earth, it uses a hybrid solar and micro-hydro power system to generate 100% of its electricity. The organic farm produces three meals a day. The internet, courtesy of a dedicated fiber line with Starlink backup, is faster than many urban co-working spaces. This is what happens when you design a workspace around nature instead of against it.

Jungle Workspace

The main workspace is a hardwood-and-screen pavilion elevated above the forest floor on stilts. Twelve dedicated desks face the canopy through floor-to-ceiling screens that let in breezes and bird calls while keeping insects out. Overhead fans circulate air. Natural light floods the space from three sides. It is simultaneously the most productive and most beautiful workspace you will ever use.

Power System

20kW solar array with 40kWh Tesla Powerwall battery bank for daytime and nighttime power. 5kW micro-hydro turbine on a nearby stream provides continuous baseload power, especially critical during rainy season when cloud cover reduces solar output. Combined system provides 100% renewable electricity 365 days a year. Biodiesel generator for emergency backup only, tested monthly.

Internet Redundancy

Primary: dedicated 80Mbps fiber line via ICE (Costa Rica's national telecom). Backup: Starlink satellite dish providing 50Mbps with automatic failover within 30 seconds. The dual system provides 99.5% uptime even in a remote jungle location. Speed tests posted weekly on the community board.

The Organic Farm

A 3-acre organic farm surrounds the workspace, producing tropical fruits, vegetables, herbs, eggs, and honey. Three daily meals are prepared from farm produce supplemented by local organic suppliers. The kitchen is zero-waste: scraps feed chickens and compost systems. Guests are welcome to participate in farm activities during work breaks.

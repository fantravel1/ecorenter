Eco Village Tulum: Off-Grid Jungle Living with Cenote Swimming and Starlink Connectivity

Live in a solar-powered jungle village where your morning commute is a walk through tropical forest, your lunch break is a swim in a crystal-clear cenote, and your evening is a Mayan temazcal ceremony under the stars. 120Mbps Starlink, three organic meals daily, and a permaculture garden you can help tend. Monthly stays from $800.

Photo by Unsplash photographer. The lush Yucatan jungle surrounding Eco Village Tulum.

The Space

The jungle is your office. The cenote is your pool.

"I was skeptical about compost toilets and Starlink internet in the jungle. Two months later, I could not imagine going back. The toilet composting system is odorless and brilliant. The Starlink is faster than my apartment WiFi in Brooklyn. And every afternoon, I swim in water so clear you can see 30 meters to the bottom. This is not roughing it. This is living intelligently."

— Rachel, Content Strategist, 3-month resident

Set on 5 acres of preserved jungle 8 kilometers inland from Tulum's beach, the Eco Village demonstrates that off-grid living is not about sacrifice but about design intelligence. A 30kW solar array with Tesla Powerwall storage provides reliable electricity. Composting toilets and constructed wetlands eliminate wastewater. A 1.5-acre permaculture garden produces over 60% of meals. And Starlink internet delivers connectivity that rivals urban fiber. The result is a community that proves the most comfortable way to live is also the most sustainable.

Jungle Workspace

The main workspace is a thatched-roof palapa with open sides facing the jungle canopy. 12 dedicated desks with ergonomic seating, bamboo monitor stands, and power strips connected to the solar array. A second, quieter palapa seats 6 for focused deep work. Hammock workstations under the trees offer a third option for casual work sessions.

Internet Infrastructure

Primary: Starlink satellite with clear sky view through a canopy gap. Secondary: Telmex 4G LTE with directional antenna. WiFi mesh network covers the entire compound. Battery backup ensures connectivity during solar intermittency. Speed tests posted weekly on the community board.

Private Cabins

Each resident has a private palapa cabin with ensuite composting toilet, outdoor garden shower, queen bed, mosquito netting, storage, and a private terrace with jungle views. Cabins are naturally ventilated with ceiling fans powered by the solar array. Construction uses local hardwoods, palm thatch, and limestone, keeping interiors cool even in peak Yucatan heat.

Community Spaces

A communal kitchen and dining palapa hosts three daily meals. A yoga and movement platform faces the sunrise. The cenote trail leads to the private swimming hole. Evening gatherings happen around a fire pit. A library palapa holds a curated collection of books on permaculture, sustainability, Mayan culture, and travel.

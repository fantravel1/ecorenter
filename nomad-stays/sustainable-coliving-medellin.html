Sustainable Co-Living Medellin: Affordable Green Living in the City of Eternal Spring

The most affordable eco nomad stay in our network, with no compromise on sustainability or connectivity. 200Mbps fiber internet, rooftop garden, active recycling program, and community projects that make you a neighbor, not a tourist. Monthly stays from $500 in one of Latin America's most innovative cities.

The Community

Being a good neighbor is not optional. It is the whole point.

"Medellin taught me that the most sustainable thing a nomad can do is stay long enough to care. When you volunteer at the neighborhood English class, when you know the names of the vendors at the market, when the abuela next door waves at you from her balcony, you are not consuming a destination. You are contributing to a community."

— Camila Rodriguez, Co-Living Community Director

Medellin's transformation from one of the world's most dangerous cities to one of its most innovative is a story of community resilience. The Sustainable Co-Living honors that story by embedding residents into the social fabric of the neighborhood. Five percent of every monthly fee funds local projects. Residents are expected to participate in at least one community activity per month. Spanish language exchange sessions connect nomads with local students. The rooftop garden produces herbs and vegetables shared with elderly neighbors. This is not a bubble. It is a bridge.

Co-Working Floor

The dedicated co-working floor features 20 desks with ergonomic chairs, adjustable monitors, and individual task lighting. Six hot desks line the window wall with mountain views. A sound-insulated phone booth handles private calls. A 6-person meeting room with whiteboard and video conferencing equipment is available for team meetings. The space is air-conditioned, though Medellin's eternal spring climate rarely requires it.

Rooftop Garden Workspace

The rooftop combines a productive garden with a casual work area. Shaded tables surrounded by herb beds and flowering plants offer open-air workspace with panoramic mountain views. The garden produces rosemary, basil, cilantro, tomatoes, and peppers used in the communal kitchen. It is simultaneously a food production space, a workspace, and a community gathering point.

Sustainability Systems

Solar panels generate 40% of the building's electricity. A comprehensive 5-bin recycling system processes all household waste. Organic kitchen waste feeds the rooftop compost system. Low-flow water fixtures reduce consumption by 35%. The communal kitchen operates a zero-food-waste policy. Cleaning products are biodegradable and locally made.

Community Projects

English tutoring for neighborhood children (twice weekly). Food sharing with elderly residents (weekly). Community garden maintenance in the local park (monthly). Spanish-English language exchange (weekly). Local market tours with cooking workshops (biweekly). All programs run by local coordinators with nomad volunteer participation.

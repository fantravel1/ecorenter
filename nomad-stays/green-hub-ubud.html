Green Hub Ubud: Work From a Bamboo Cathedral Overlooking Rice Terraces

Your office is an open-air bamboo pavilion surrounded by emerald rice terraces and the sounds of water flowing through ancient irrigation channels. 150Mbps fiber keeps you connected while permaculture gardens feed you. Monthly stays from $600.

The Space

This is what happens when you build a workspace the way nature builds a forest.

"The bamboo walls breathe. The roof channels rainwater into the garden. The toilets make compost. The kitchen serves food grown twenty meters from your desk. Working here makes you realize how disconnected most workspaces are from the systems that sustain life. Here, you are part of the cycle."

— Wayan, Green Hub Community Manager

The Green Hub is built entirely from locally harvested bamboo using traditional Balinese construction techniques updated with modern engineering. The multi-level bamboo pavilion houses co-working spaces, a library, meeting pods, and a communal kitchen. Surrounding the workspace, a 2-acre permaculture garden produces 60% of the food served in the communal kitchen. Composting toilets save 30,000 liters of water annually. Rainwater harvesting provides irrigation and non-potable water. Every system is designed to demonstrate that productivity and ecological responsibility can coexist beautifully.

Main Pavilion Workspace

The two-story bamboo pavilion features 24 workstations with bamboo desks, ergonomic chairs, and power outlets. The upper level catches afternoon breezes and overlooks the rice terraces. The lower level is shaded and cooler during midday heat. Natural ventilation eliminates the need for air conditioning except during the hottest weeks. Ethernet drops available at 12 dedicated desks.

Garden Work Pods

Four individual bamboo pods scattered through the permaculture garden offer private workspace surrounded by tropical plants, bird calls, and the sound of flowing water from the subak irrigation channels. Each pod has WiFi, power, and a small desk. Perfect for deep focus work or private calls.

Natural Swimming Pool

A chemical-free natural swimming pool filtered by aquatic plants provides a refreshing break between work sessions. The pool terrace has lounge seating for casual work with laptops. Adjacent changing rooms use solar-heated water for showers.

Seasonal Considerations

During wet season (Nov-Mar), afternoon rain is common but typically lasts 1-2 hours. The covered pavilion workspace is fully protected. Some garden pods may be unusable during heavy downpours. Humidity increases but overhead fans and natural cross-ventilation keep the workspace comfortable. Dry season (Apr-Oct) offers ideal open-air working conditions.
